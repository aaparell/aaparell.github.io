Best Shoes To Wear For Diabetic Neuropathy

Written by Luffy Aug 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

If you have diabetic neuropathy, you may experience numbness, tingling, or pain in your feet. This can make it difficult to find shoes that are comfortable and supportive. Wearing the wrong shoes can lead to foot injuries, infections, and other complications. In this blog post, we'll explore the best shoes to wear for diabetic neuropathy.

Living with diabetic neuropathy can be difficult. You may have trouble walking or standing for long periods of time. You may also be at risk for foot injuries and infections. Finding the right shoes can help alleviate some of these issues.

The best shoes for diabetic neuropathy are those that provide support and cushioning. Look for shoes with a wide toe box, a firm heel counter, and extra padding in the insole. Shoes made from breathable materials like leather or mesh can also help keep your feet dry and comfortable.

In summary, the best shoes to wear for diabetic neuropathy are those that are supportive, cushioned, and made from breathable materials. These shoes can help alleviate pain and reduce the risk of foot injuries and infections.

Orthopedic Shoes

Orthopedic shoes are designed to provide support and cushioning for people with foot problems like diabetic neuropathy. These shoes have a wide toe box, a firm heel counter, and extra padding in the insole. They also come in a variety of styles and colors, so you can find a pair that fits your personal style.

Personally, I have found orthopedic shoes to be the most comfortable option for my diabetic neuropathy. They provide the support and cushioning I need to walk and stand for long periods of time without experiencing pain or discomfort.

Diabetic Socks

In addition to wearing the right shoes, it's important to wear the right socks. Diabetic socks are designed to reduce pressure and friction on your feet, which can help prevent foot injuries and infections. These socks are usually made from moisture-wicking materials and have a non-binding top to prevent them from digging into your skin.

I wear diabetic socks every day and have noticed a significant improvement in my foot health. My feet are less prone to blisters and infections, and I no longer experience as much pain and discomfort when walking or standing for long periods of time.

Custom Orthotics

If you have severe diabetic neuropathy, your doctor may recommend custom orthotics. These are shoe inserts that are made specifically for your feet. They can provide additional support and cushioning to help alleviate pain and reduce the risk of foot injuries and infections.

Custom orthotics can be expensive, but they may be covered by insurance. Talk to your doctor to see if custom orthotics are a good option for you.

Proper Fit

No matter what type of shoes you choose, it's important to make sure they fit properly. Shoes that are too tight or too loose can cause foot injuries and infections. When trying on shoes, make sure there is enough room in the toe box for your toes to move freely. The heel should be snug, but not tight. Walk around in the shoes to make sure they feel comfortable and supportive.

Personally, I have found that getting my feet measured by a professional shoe fitter is the best way to ensure a proper fit. They can recommend shoes that are appropriate for my foot size and shape, as well as my diabetic neuropathy.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I wear sandals if I have diabetic neuropathy?

A: It's best to avoid sandals if you have diabetic neuropathy. Sandals don't provide enough support or cushioning for your feet, which can increase the risk of foot injuries and infections.

Q: How often should I replace my shoes?

A: It's a good idea to replace your shoes every six months to a year, depending on how often you wear them. Over time, shoes can lose their support and cushioning, which can make them less effective for managing diabetic neuropathy.

Q: Can I wear high heels if I have diabetic neuropathy?

A: It's best to avoid high heels if you have diabetic neuropathy. High heels can cause pressure and strain on your feet, which can increase the risk of foot injuries and infections.

Q: Can I wear athletic shoes if I have diabetic neuropathy?

A: Yes, athletic shoes can be a good option for people with diabetic neuropathy. Look for shoes with a wide toe box, a firm heel counter, and extra padding in the insole. Shoes made from breathable materials like mesh can also help keep your feet dry and comfortable.

Conclusion of Best Shoes to Wear for Diabetic Neuropathy

Finding the right shoes can make a big difference in managing diabetic neuropathy. The best shoes are those that provide support, cushioning, and breathability. Orthopedic shoes, diabetic socks, custom orthotics, and properly fitting shoes are all great options for people with diabetic neuropathy. By wearing the right shoes, you can reduce the risk of foot injuries and infections, and improve your overall foot health.
