Discovering The Names Of Kalabari Traditional Attire

Written by Bardi Jun 09, 2023 · 4 min read

Have you ever been to a Kalabari traditional wedding or event and found yourself struggling to identify the names of the different attires worn by guests? It can be quite embarrassing to ask around for the names of the attires, and it makes you feel like an outsider. In this post, we will be discussing the names of Kalabari traditional attire in detail, so you can attend events with confidence and knowledge.

Attending Kalabari traditional events can be a great way to experience the rich culture and tradition of the Kalabari people. However, one of the challenges that attendees face is identifying the names of the different attires worn by guests. This can be quite daunting, especially for first-timers who are not familiar with the names of the attires.

If you're attending a Kalabari traditional event, it is essential to know the names of the different attires to avoid feeling out of place. Furthermore, knowing the names of the attires will enable you to appreciate the rich culture and history behind each attire, making your experience at the event more meaningful and enjoyable.

In summary, the names of Kalabari traditional attire are essential to know when attending traditional events. It helps you appreciate the culture and history behind each attire and avoid feeling out of place.

1. Isi-Agwu

The Isi-Agwu is a traditional Kalabari attire worn by chiefs and high-ranking individuals in the community. The attire consists of a long robe, usually made of high-quality fabric, and adorned with gold or silver embroidery. It also features a cap made of the same material as the robe and is usually paired with a walking stick.

My grandfather was a chief in our community, and I remember seeing him wear the Isi-Agwu on several occasions during traditional events. It was always a sight to behold, and he looked regal and dignified.

2. Ekpo Masquerade Attire

The Ekpo Masquerade Attire is a traditional Kalabari attire worn by members of the Ekpo secret society during traditional events. The attire consists of a full-body costume made of raffia or other natural materials, with a wooden mask and a colorful headdress.

During a traditional event I attended, I had the opportunity to witness the Ekpo Masquerade dance. It was a mesmerizing sight to see the dancers move in unison, and the colorful attire added to the overall spectacle.

3. Omu-Tutu

The Omu-Tutu is a traditional Kalabari attire worn by women during traditional events. The attire consists of a long, flowing gown made of colorful fabric, adorned with intricate embroidery and beading. It is usually paired with a matching headscarf and jewelry.

My mother owns an Omu-Tutu attire, and I always admired the intricate details and vibrant colors of the dress. It was always a sight to see her wear it during traditional events.

4. Igo Agbada

The Igo Agbada is a traditional Kalabari attire worn by men during traditional events. The attire consists of a long robe made of high-quality fabric, adorned with intricate embroidery and beading. It is usually paired with matching trousers and a cap.

During a traditional event I attended, I saw several men wearing the Igo Agbada, and it was fascinating to see the different designs and colors of the attire.

Question and Answer

Q. What is the significance of the Isi-Agwu attire?

A. The Isi-Agwu attire is a symbol of authority and power among the Kalabari people. It is worn by chiefs and high-ranking individuals in the community.

Q. Who wears the Ekpo Masquerade Attire?

A. The Ekpo Masquerade Attire is worn by members of the Ekpo secret society during traditional events.

Q. What is the Omu-Tutu attire?

A. The Omu-Tutu is a traditional Kalabari attire worn by women during traditional events. It consists of a long, flowing gown made of colorful fabric, adorned with intricate embroidery and beading.

Q. What is the Igo Agbada attire?

A. The Igo Agbada is a traditional Kalabari attire worn by men during traditional events. It consists of a long robe made of high-quality fabric, adorned with intricate embroidery and beading.

Conclusion of Names of Kalabari Traditional Attire

Knowing the names of Kalabari traditional attire is essential when attending traditional events. It enables you to appreciate the culture and history behind each attire and avoid feeling out of place. The Isi-Agwu, Ekpo Masquerade Attire, Omu-Tutu, and Igo Agbada are some of the popular traditional attires worn by the Kalabari people, and understanding their significance adds to the overall experience of attending traditional events.
