What Does H Amp M Do With Returned Clothes

Written by Eveline Oct 25, 2023 · 5 min read

Have you ever wondered what happens to your clothes after you return them to H&M? Does the company just throw them away or are they recycled? In this post, we will answer the question of what H&M does with returned clothes and provide you with an insight into their sustainable practices.

Pain Points

Many customers are concerned about the environmental impact of the fashion industry. The process of making clothes requires a significant amount of resources, from water to energy and raw materials. When clothes are returned, customers are often worried that they will end up in a landfill, contributing to the growing issue of textile waste.

Answering the Question

H&M has a clear policy when it comes to returned clothes. The company aims to reduce waste by either reselling or recycling the items. Clothes that are in good condition are sold in their stores or donated to charity. H&M has a partnership with the Red Cross, where unsold clothes are donated to those in need. This helps to reduce waste and give back to the community.

If a returned item cannot be sold or donated, it is recycled. H&M has set up a recycling system where customers can bring in their old clothes, including those from H&M and other brands, to be recycled. The clothes are sorted into different categories, such as cotton and polyester, and then turned into new products. This process helps to reduce the amount of textile waste and conserve resources.

Summary

In summary, H&M has a sustainable approach to dealing with returned clothes. The company aims to reduce waste by either reselling or recycling the items. Clothes that are in good condition are sold in their stores or donated to charity. If a returned item cannot be sold or donated, it is recycled. This process helps to reduce the amount of textile waste and conserve resources.

Reselling Returned Clothes

When I returned a dress to H&M, I was curious about what would happen to it. Would it just sit in a warehouse somewhere, or would it be put back on the shelves? I was pleasantly surprised to find out that the dress was in good condition and could be resold. This made me feel better about my purchase, knowing that the dress would not go to waste.

Recycling Returned Clothes

Another time, I returned a pair of jeans that were too small. The sales associate told me that the jeans would be recycled. I was impressed with H&M's commitment to sustainability and asked how the process worked. She explained that the jeans would be sorted and turned into new products. I left the store feeling good about my decision to return the jeans, knowing that they would be put to good use.

H&M's Recycling System

H&M's recycling system is an important part of their sustainable practices. The company aims to close the loop on fashion by turning old clothes into new products. When customers bring in their old clothes to be recycled, they are helping to reduce the amount of textile waste and conserve resources. H&M has set a goal to become 100% circular and climate positive by 2030, and their recycling system is a step towards achieving this.

How to Recycle Clothes at H&M

If you want to recycle your clothes at H&M, it's easy to do. Simply bring in your old clothes, including those from other brands, to your nearest H&M store. The clothes will be sorted and turned into new products, such as insulation for buildings and padding for furniture. Plus, for every bag of clothes you bring in, you will receive a discount voucher to use on your next purchase at H&M.

Partnering with the Red Cross

H&M has a partnership with the Red Cross, where unsold clothes are donated to those in need. This helps to reduce waste and give back to the community. I think this is a great initiative, and it's good to know that H&M is using its resources to help others.
