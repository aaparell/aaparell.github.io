Types of Indian Designer Mens Kurta

There are different types of Indian designer mens kurta to choose from, each with its unique style and purpose. Some of the most common types of kurta include:

Straight Kurta

As the name suggests, this type of kurta has a straight cut and is long in length. It is a traditional style that is perfect for formal occasions such as weddings and religious events. It is usually made of silk or cotton fabric and can be paired with churidar, pajama, or dhoti.

Angrakha Kurta

This type of kurta has a unique style that resembles a robe. It has a diagonal opening that overlaps and is tied with strings or buttons. It is a perfect option for festive occasions and can be paired with churidar, pajama, or dhoti.

Bundi Kurta

This type of kurta is a fusion of western and Indian style. It has a jacket-like appearance with a short length kurta and a sleeveless jacket on top. It is perfect for semi-formal occasions and can be paired with jeans, churidar, or pajama.

How to Choose the Right Indian Designer Mens Kurta

Choosing the right Indian designer mens kurta can be a daunting task, especially if you are not familiar with the different types of kurta and fabrics. Here are some tips to help you choose the perfect kurta for your needs:

Know your body type: Choose a kurta that suits your body type. If you are tall and slim, opt for a straight cut kurta. If you are short and stocky, choose a fitted kurta with a jacket.

Choose the right fabric: The fabric used in kurta plays a significant role in its comfort and style. Choose a fabric that suits the occasion and weather. For example, silk and cotton are perfect for formal occasions, while linen and khadi are great for casual events.

Consider the occasion: Choose a kurta that suits the occasion. For formal events, opt for a traditional silk or cotton kurta, while for casual events, choose a printed or embroidered kurta.

Frequently Asked Questions about Indian Designer Mens Kurta

Q: What is the difference between a kurta and a sherwani?

A: A kurta is a long shirt that is worn with churidar, pajama, or dhoti. A sherwani is a long coat-like garment that is worn over a kurta and paired with churidar or pajama.

Q: Can I wear a kurta to work?

A: Yes, you can wear a kurta to work, but make sure it is appropriate for the workplace. Choose a simple and subtle kurta that is not too flashy or overly embroidered.

Q: How do I care for my kurta?

A: Most kurta can be hand washed or dry cleaned. Always read the care label before washing your kurta. Avoid using bleach or harsh detergents, and always air dry your kurta.

Q: Can I wear a kurta with jeans?

A: Yes, you can wear a kurta with jeans, but make sure it is a casual kurta that is not too formal or traditional. Opt for a printed or embroidered kurta and pair it with light wash jeans.
