The Best Mezcal Brands In Mexico A Guide For Mezcal Lovers

Written by William Jul 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you a mezcal lover searching for the best mezcal brands in Mexico? Look no further! In this guide, we will explore some of the top mezcal brands in Mexico, their unique features, and what makes them stand out.

Mezcal is a traditional Mexican alcoholic beverage made from the agave plant. It has a unique and rich flavor that has garnered a loyal following worldwide. However, with so many brands out there, it can be challenging to find the best one that suits your taste and budget.

In this guide, we will help you navigate the world of mezcal by highlighting some of the best brands and what makes them special. Whether you're a seasoned mezcal aficionado or a newcomer to the drink, this guide will help you choose the right brand for your palate.

Del Maguey Mezcal

One of the most popular and respected mezcal brands in Mexico is Del Maguey. This brand stands out for its artisanal production process, which involves using traditional techniques and hand-mashing the agave. Del Maguey offers a wide variety of mezcal flavors, from fruity to smoky, making it a versatile brand that caters to different tastes.

I remember the first time I tried Del Maguey mezcal. It was at a small bar in Oaxaca, and I was blown away by the rich flavor and smokiness. I've been a fan ever since.

Los Danzantes Mezcal

Another top mezcal brand in Mexico is Los Danzantes. This brand is known for its commitment to sustainability and ethical production practices. Los Danzantes mezcal is made from certified organic agave, and the company works closely with local farmers to ensure fair wages and sustainable farming practices.

When I visited the Los Danzantes distillery, I was impressed by their dedication to sustainability. The entire production process was designed to minimize waste and reduce the brand's environmental impact. Plus, the mezcal tasted fantastic!

Casa Noble Mezcal

Casa Noble is another top mezcal brand in Mexico that is well-known for its premium quality and taste. This brand uses a slow-roasting process that gives the mezcal a unique and complex flavor profile. Casa Noble mezcal is aged in oak barrels, which adds a smoothness and richness to the drink.

If you're looking for a high-end mezcal that is perfect for sipping, Casa Noble is an excellent choice. The brand's commitment to quality and taste is evident in every bottle.

How to Choose the Best Mezcal Brand for You

Choosing the best mezcal brand for you can be a challenging task, especially if you're new to the drink. Here are some things to keep in mind when selecting a brand:

1. Look for brands that use traditional production methods and hand-mash their agave. These brands tend to have a richer and more complex flavor profile.

2. Consider the flavor profile you prefer. Mezcal can range from fruity and sweet to smoky and earthy. Try different brands and flavors to find the one that suits your palate.

3. Check the brand's sustainability and ethical practices. Many top mezcal brands in Mexico are committed to reducing their environmental impact and supporting local farmers.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the difference between mezcal and tequila?

A: While both are made from agave, mezcal is produced from a wider variety of agave plants and has a smokier flavor profile than tequila.

Q: How should I drink mezcal?

A: Mezcal is traditionally sipped neat, but it can also be used in cocktails or mixed with lime and salt.

Q: How long does mezcal last?

A: Mezcal can last for several years if stored properly in a cool, dark place. Once opened, it should be consumed within a few months.

Q: What food pairs well with mezcal?

A: Mezcal pairs well with spicy and savory dishes, such as grilled meats, tacos, and ceviche.

Conclusion of Best Mezcal Brands in Mexico

Choosing the best mezcal brand for you is all about finding the right balance of taste, quality, and ethical production practices. Whether you prefer a fruity or smoky flavor, there is a mezcal out there that will suit your palate. By trying different brands and exploring the world of mezcal, you can find a drink that you will love and appreciate for years to come.
