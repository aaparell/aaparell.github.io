How To Put Laces In Nike Shoes A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Oct 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you struggling to put laces in your Nike shoes? It can be frustrating when you can't get them just right. Maybe the laces keep coming undone or the bow doesn't look quite right. Whatever your experience, we've got you covered with this step-by-step guide on how to put laces in Nike shoes.

Putting laces in Nike shoes can be a pain point for many people. It can be time-consuming and frustrating to get them just right. But having properly laced shoes is important for comfort, support, and style. Plus, you don't want to be tripping over untied laces all day.

First, make sure you have the right size laces for your shoes. Most Nike shoes come with laces, but if you need to replace them, make sure to get the right length. You can find this information on the packaging or by measuring your old laces.

Next, remove the old laces from your shoes. You can do this by untying them and pulling them out of the eyelets. Take note of how they were laced so you can recreate the same pattern with your new laces.

Step-by-Step Guide for Lacing Nike Shoes

Step 1: Begin at the Bottom

Start by inserting one end of the lace into the bottom eyelet on one side of the shoe. Pull the lace through so that both ends are even.

Pro tip:

You can use a lace threading tool to make this step easier.

Step 2: Criss-Cross the Laces

Take the left end of the lace and insert it into the eyelet directly above it on the right side of the shoe. Then, take the right end of the lace and insert it into the eyelet directly above it on the left side of the shoe.

Pro tip:

Make sure the laces are not twisted or tangled as you criss-cross them.

Step 3: Continue Criss-Crossing

Repeat step 2 until you reach the top of the shoe. Make sure to pull the laces taut after each criss-cross.

Pro tip:

You can adjust the tension of the laces to fit the shoe to your foot by pulling tighter or looser as needed.

Step 4: Tie the Laces

When you reach the top of the shoe, tie the laces in a bow or knot. Make sure the bow is tight enough to keep the shoe secure but not too tight that it cuts off circulation.

Additional Lacing Techniques

If you're feeling adventurous, try out some different lacing techniques to add some flair to your Nike shoes. Here are a few options:

Loop Lacing:

This technique creates a looped pattern on the top of the shoe. Start by lacing the shoe normally up to the second-to-last eyelet. Instead of crossing over to the opposite side, loop the lace around to the next eyelet on the same side. Repeat until you reach the top of the shoe and tie the laces as usual.

Checkerboard Lacing:

This technique creates a checkerboard pattern on the top of the shoe. Start by lacing the shoe normally up to the second-to-last eyelet. Instead of crossing over to the opposite side, bring the lace straight up to the next eyelet on the same side. Then, cross over to the opposite side and bring the lace straight up to the next eyelet on that side. Repeat until you reach the top of the shoe and tie the laces as usual.

Question and Answer

Q: How do I know if I have the right size laces?

A: You can check the packaging of the laces or measure your old laces to make sure you get the right length.

Q: Can I use different colors of laces on my Nike shoes?

A: Yes, you can use any color or pattern of laces that you like.

Q: What do I do if my laces keep coming undone?

A: Make sure to tie the bow or knot tightly and double knot if needed. You can also try a different lacing technique to keep the laces more secure.

Q: Can I use this guide for other types of shoes?

A: Yes, this guide can be used for most types of shoes that have eyelets.

Conclusion of How to Put Laces in Nike Shoes

Putting laces in Nike shoes can be time-consuming, but it's important for comfort, support, and style. By following this step-by-step guide, you can easily lace up your Nike shoes and even try out some different lacing techniques. With properly laced shoes, you'll be ready to take on any activity with ease.
