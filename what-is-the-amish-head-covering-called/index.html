The Amish are a religious group that follow a strict set of beliefs and practices. One of these practices is the wearing of a head covering, which is worn by both men and women. The head covering is an important part of their culture and represents modesty, humility, and submission to God's will.

The Amish head covering is called a "prayer covering" or "kapp" in their language, which is Pennsylvania Dutch. The term "prayer covering" is fitting because it is worn during times of prayer and worship. The kapp is a simple, white, bonnet-style head covering that is tied under the chin, and is worn by women and girls. Men and boys wear a wide-brimmed hat made of straw or felt.

So, to summarize, the Amish head covering is called a "prayer covering" or "kapp" and is worn by both men and women as a symbol of modesty, humility, and submission to God's will.
