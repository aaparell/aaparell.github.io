How To Put Your Shoelaces On Vans A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby May 25, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you struggle with putting your shoelaces on vans? Fret not, you're not alone! Many people find it challenging to lace up their vans in a way that looks neat and stylish. But don't worry, with this step-by-step guide, you'll be able to put your shoelaces on vans like a pro in no time.

Putting your shoelaces on vans may seem like a simple task, but it can be frustrating if you don't know how to do it correctly. The knots may come undone, and the laces may look uneven, making your shoes look untidy. However, with the right technique, you can avoid these problems and ensure your shoes always look great.

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to put your shoelaces on vans:

Step 1: Thread the Laces

Start by threading your laces through the bottom eyelets of your vans. Pull the laces through until they are even in length.

Step 2: Cross the Laces

Take your left lace and cross it over the right lace, forming an 'X' shape. Take the end of the left lace and thread it through the eyelet on the right side, pulling it through until it is snug.

Step 3: Create a Loop

Take the end of the right lace and create a loop by folding it over itself. Hold the loop with your left hand.

Step 4: Thread the Left Lace

Take the end of the left lace and thread it over the loop and through the hole. Pull the lace tight, so the loop is snug against the shoe.

Step 5: Complete the Knot

Repeat steps 3 and 4 on the other side, creating another loop with the right lace and threading the left lace through it. Pull the laces tight, and you've got yourself a neat and tidy knot.

That's it! With these five simple steps, you can put your shoelaces on vans without any hassle.

Why Is It Important to Lace Your Vans Correctly?

Lacing your vans correctly not only makes them look stylish but also ensures they fit comfortably. Uneven laces can lead to discomfort and even pain if they rub against your skin. Proper lacing also prevents the laces from coming undone during the day, which can be frustrating and embarrassing.

How to Lace Your Vans for Different Looks

There are various ways to lace your vans to achieve different looks. You can try the straight bar lacing for a clean and classic look or the checkerboard lacing for a unique and eye-catching style. Experiment with different lacing techniques to find the one that suits you best.

FAQs

Q1: Can I use any shoelaces for my vans?

A1: Yes, you can use any shoelaces that fit through the eyelets of your vans. However, it's best to use laces made specifically for sneakers, as they are durable and can withstand wear and tear.

Q2: How tight should I tie my shoelaces?

A2: Your shoelaces should be tight enough to keep your feet secure in your shoes but not so tight that they restrict blood flow or cause discomfort.

Q3: How often should I replace my shoelaces?

A3: It's a good idea to replace your shoelaces every six months to a year, depending on how frequently you wear your shoes. Worn-out laces can lead to discomfort and make your shoes look untidy.

Q4: Can I wash my shoelaces?

A4: Yes, you can wash your shoelaces by hand or in a washing machine. Use a mild detergent and cold water to avoid shrinking or damaging the laces.

Conclusion

Putting your shoelaces on vans is easy when you know the right technique. By following this step-by-step guide, you can ensure your laces are always neat and tidy, giving your shoes a stylish look. Experiment with different lacing techniques to find the one that suits your style best.
