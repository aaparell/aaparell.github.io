Are You Supposed To Wear Underwear Under Bike Shorts

Written by Christine Jun 26, 2023 · 4 min read

If you're new to cycling, you've probably wondered whether you should wear underwear under your bike shorts. Maybe you've tried it and found it uncomfortable, or maybe you've heard conflicting advice from other cyclists. In this post, we'll answer the question once and for all and give you some tips for a comfortable ride.

Many cyclists experience discomfort when wearing underwear under their bike shorts. This is because the chamois, the padded insert in the shorts, is designed to be worn directly against your skin. Underwear can bunch up, causing chafing and irritation, and it can also trap sweat, leading to bacterial infections.

The short answer is no, you're not supposed to wear underwear under bike shorts. The chamois is designed to be worn against your skin, and adding another layer can cause discomfort and even lead to health problems. If you're worried about hygiene, you can wash your bike shorts after every ride or wear a clean pair each time.

In summary, here are the main points to remember:

  • Wearing underwear under bike shorts can cause discomfort and health problems.
  • The chamois is designed to be worn directly against your skin.
  • Wash your bike shorts after every ride or wear a clean pair each time.

Why Do Some Cyclists Wear Underwear Under Bike Shorts?

While it's not recommended, some cyclists choose to wear underwear under their bike shorts for various reasons. Some may find it more comfortable, while others may wear it for modesty reasons. However, if you find yourself uncomfortable wearing underwear under your bike shorts, it's best to switch to cycling-specific shorts and skip the underwear.

What Are the Benefits of Wearing Cycling-Specific Shorts?

Cycling-specific shorts are designed to provide maximum comfort and performance during a ride. The chamois is strategically placed to cushion your sit bones and reduce pressure on sensitive areas. The shorts are also made from moisture-wicking materials that keep you dry and comfortable, even during long rides. By wearing cycling-specific shorts, you'll reduce the risk of chafing, irritation, and bacterial infections.

How to Choose the Right Bike Shorts?

When choosing bike shorts, there are several factors to consider. First, look for shorts with a high-quality chamois that's designed for your gender and riding style. The shorts should fit snugly but not be too tight or restrictive. Look for shorts made from breathable, moisture-wicking materials that will keep you comfortable on long rides. Finally, consider the length and style of the shorts – some cyclists prefer bib shorts for added support, while others prefer traditional waistband shorts.

How to Care for Your Bike Shorts?

To prolong the life of your bike shorts, it's essential to care for them properly. Wash them after every ride in cold water with a mild detergent, and avoid using fabric softeners or bleach. Hang the shorts to dry, and avoid using a dryer, which can damage the chamois and shorten the life of the shorts. Store the shorts flat or folded, and avoid leaving them crumpled in your bag or drawer.

My Personal Experience

When I first started cycling, I wore underwear under my bike shorts because I didn't know any better. I quickly realized that it was causing discomfort and chafing, and I switched to cycling-specific shorts. Since then, I've never looked back – my rides are more comfortable, and I haven't experienced any health problems. I highly recommend investing in a good pair of cycling shorts and skipping the underwear.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Can I wear underwear under my cycling tights?

A: No, it's not recommended to wear underwear under cycling tights for the same reasons as bike shorts. The chamois is designed to be worn directly against your skin.

Q: How often should I wash my bike shorts?

A: It's recommended to wash your bike shorts after every ride to prevent bacterial infections and prolong the life of the shorts.

Q: What if I don't like the feel of the chamois against my skin?

A: If you find the chamois uncomfortable, try wearing a different brand or style of bike shorts. Some cyclists prefer bib shorts for added support, while others prefer traditional waistband shorts. You can also try using chamois cream to reduce friction and irritation.

Q: What if I can't afford expensive cycling shorts?

A: While high-end cycling shorts can be expensive, there are many affordable options available. Look for shorts with a quality chamois and breathable, moisture-wicking materials. You can also check online retailers or discount stores for deals and sales.

Conclusion of Are You Supposed to Wear Underwear Under Bike Shorts

In conclusion, you're not supposed to wear underwear under bike shorts. The chamois is designed to be worn directly against your skin, and adding another layer can cause discomfort and health problems. By investing in a good pair of cycling-specific shorts and caring for them properly, you'll enjoy a comfortable and healthy ride.
