Why Do Indian Brides Wear Red

Written by Daniel Aug 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Indian weddings are known for their vibrant colors, ornate decorations, and elaborate rituals. One of the most striking aspects of an Indian wedding is the attire worn by the bride, especially the color red. The question that often arises is, why does Indian brides wear red? In this blog post, we will explore the reasons behind this tradition and its significance.

For many brides, choosing the perfect wedding outfit is a significant part of their wedding planning process. They carefully select a dress that reflects their personal style and cultural traditions. However, the color red is a constant presence in most Indian wedding trousseaus. This choice of color is not arbitrary and has a deep-rooted significance in Indian culture.

Symbolism of Red in Indian Culture

In Indian culture, the color red holds immense significance. It is considered to be a symbol of purity, sensuality, and prosperity. Red is also believed to ward off evil and protect the bride from harm. Moreover, it is seen as a representation of the rising sun, which denotes a new beginning.

Wearing red on the wedding day is a tradition that dates back to ancient times. It is believed that goddess Parvati, the epitome of beauty and feminine power, wore a red saree on her wedding day to Lord Shiva. This incident set the precedent for future brides to follow in her footsteps and wear red on their wedding day.

Personal Experience

As an Indian woman, I can attest to the significance of the color red in our culture. I remember watching my grandmother and mother getting ready for weddings, and the color red was always a part of their attire. When it was my turn to get married, I knew that I wanted to honor this tradition by choosing a red lehenga as my wedding outfit. The color gave me a sense of confidence and made me feel connected to my roots.

Wedding Rituals and the Color Red

The color red also plays a significant role in various wedding rituals. For instance, during the engagement ceremony, the bride is presented with a red sari or an outfit in a shade of red by the groom's family. This gesture symbolizes the acceptance of the bride into the groom's family. Similarly, during the wedding ceremony, the bride is adorned in red from head to toe, including her jewelry and henna designs.

The Science behind the Color Red

Research has shown that the color red can have a psychological impact on our emotions and behavior. It is believed to increase feelings of attraction, passion, and confidence. These qualities align with the significance of the color red in Indian culture, making it an ideal choice for bridal wear.

How to Incorporate Red into Your Wedding Attire

If you are an Indian bride-to-be and want to incorporate the color red into your wedding attire, there are several ways to do so. You can opt for a red lehenga, saree, or even a red dupatta paired with a different colored outfit. You can also accessorize with red jewelry, such as bangles, earrings, and necklaces.

Personal Experience

My sister recently got married, and she chose to wear a red and gold lehenga for her wedding. The outfit was beautifully embroidered and had intricate details that made it stand out. She paired it with traditional gold jewelry and red bangles, which completed the look perfectly.

Question and Answer

Q. Why is the color red considered auspicious in Indian weddings?

A. The color red is considered auspicious in Indian weddings because it signifies purity, sensuality, and prosperity. It is also believed to ward off evil and protect the bride from harm.

Q. Is it necessary for Indian brides to wear red on their wedding day?

A. No, it is not necessary for Indian brides to wear red on their wedding day. However, it is a cultural tradition that many brides choose to honor.

Q. What are some other colors that Indian brides wear?

A. Indian brides may also wear colors such as pink, orange, green, and gold. These colors have different significances and may be chosen based on personal preference or cultural traditions.

Q. Can non-Indian brides wear red on their wedding day?

A. Yes, non-Indian brides can wear red on their wedding day if they choose to do so. The color red is universally associated with love and passion and can be a beautiful choice for bridal wear.

Conclusion of Why Does Indian Brides Wear Red

Indian weddings are steeped in tradition and culture. The choice of wedding attire is not just a fashion statement but also a reflection of cultural beliefs and values. The color red has been an integral part of Indian weddings since ancient times, and its significance continues to hold relevance even today. Whether you choose to wear red or not, it is essential to honor and respect the cultural traditions that make Indian weddings so unique and beautiful.
