What Is A Mennonite Head Covering Called

Written by Christine Aug 21, 2023 · 5 min read

For those who are not familiar with Mennonite culture, the idea of a head covering may seem foreign. However, understanding what a Mennonite head covering is and why it is important to those who wear it can be fascinating. If you are curious about what a Mennonite head covering is called and why it is worn, keep reading.

For many Mennonite women, wearing a head covering is a symbol of their faith and a way to show humility and submission to God. It is also a way to separate themselves from the secular world and to maintain their distinctive cultural identity. However, choosing to wear a head covering is a personal choice and not all Mennonite women choose to do so.

The traditional Mennonite head covering is called a prayer covering or a kapp. The kapp is typically a white or black bonnet that covers the head and ties under the chin. It is made of a lightweight fabric such as cotton or linen and is often adorned with lace or embroidery. The prayer covering is worn during church services and other religious events, as well as during daily activities such as cooking, cleaning, and caring for children.

In summary, a Mennonite head covering is called a prayer covering or a kapp. It is a symbolic item worn by many Mennonite women as a way to show their faith, humility, and cultural identity.

Why Do Mennonite Women Wear a Head Covering?

As mentioned earlier, Mennonite women wear a head covering as a symbol of their faith and a way to show humility and submission to God. However, the practice of wearing a head covering is not exclusive to the Mennonite faith. In fact, many other religious groups such as the Amish, Hutterites, and some conservative Christian denominations also require women to wear a head covering.

Personally, as a Mennonite woman, I wear a kapp as a way to connect with my cultural and religious heritage. It is also a way to show respect for God and to honor the traditions of my ancestors.

What is the Significance of the Prayer Covering?

The significance of the prayer covering varies depending on the individual and their interpretation of scripture. However, for many Mennonite women, the prayer covering is seen as a symbol of their submission to God and their willingness to follow his commands. It is also a way to show respect for authority and to maintain a sense of modesty and purity.

How is a Kapp Different from a Bonnet?

While a kapp and a bonnet may look similar, there are some key differences between the two. A kapp is typically made of a lightweight fabric such as cotton or linen and is worn snugly to the head. It ties under the chin and covers the forehead, ears, and neck. A bonnet, on the other hand, is usually made of a heavier fabric such as wool or silk and is worn more loosely on the head. It often has a brim or visor to shield the face from the sun.

How Do You Tie a Kapp?

Tying a kapp can take some practice, but it is fairly simple once you get the hang of it. To tie a kapp, start by placing the bonnet over your head with the strings hanging down on either side of your face. Bring the strings under your chin and tie them in a bow at the nape of your neck. Adjust the kapp so that it covers your forehead, ears, and neck, and you're done!

My Personal Experience with Wearing a Kapp

As a Mennonite woman, wearing a kapp is a part of my daily routine. It is a way for me to feel connected to my faith and my cultural heritage. While it can be uncomfortable at times, especially during hot summer months, I wouldn't want to go without it. Wearing a kapp is a reminder to me of the values and traditions that are important to me and my community.

Question and Answer

A: No, wearing a head covering is a personal choice and not all Mennonite women choose to do so.

Q: Can non-Mennonite women wear a kapp?

A: While non-Mennonite women are welcome to wear a kapp, it is important to understand the significance of the prayer covering and to respect the traditions of those who wear it as a part of their faith and cultural identity.

Q: Are there different styles of kapps?

A: Yes, there are many different styles of kapps that vary by region and individual preference. Some are plain and simple, while others are adorned with lace or embroidery.

Q: Is it disrespectful to ask a Mennonite woman about her kapp?

A: It is always best to approach a conversation with respect and sensitivity. If you are genuinely curious about someone's kapp, it is okay to ask politely and respectfully.

Conclusion

In conclusion, a Mennonite head covering is called a prayer covering or a kapp. It is a symbolic item worn by many Mennonite women as a way to show their faith, humility, and cultural identity. While it may seem foreign to those outside of the Mennonite community, understanding the significance of the prayer covering can be a fascinating glimpse into the values and traditions of this unique culture.
