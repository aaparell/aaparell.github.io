Indian Wedding Dress Name A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Nov 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Indian weddings are known for their grandeur and extravagance. One of the most important aspects of an Indian wedding is the attire worn by the bride and groom. The Indian wedding dress name is a topic of great interest for those attending or planning an Indian wedding. In this blog post, we will provide a comprehensive guide on Indian wedding dress names and their significance.

Choosing the right Indian wedding dress can be a daunting task for many people. There are so many different styles and designs to choose from, and each has its own unique name. Furthermore, the choice of dress can vary depending on the region, religion, and culture of the bride and groom. This can lead to confusion and stress for those not familiar with Indian wedding attire.

The target of Indian wedding dress name is to provide clarity and understanding of the different types of dresses worn by brides and grooms during Indian weddings. By the end of this guide, readers should have a better understanding of the significance of each dress and its name.

In summary, this guide will cover the different types of Indian wedding dresses and their names, the significance of each dress, and how to choose the right dress for your wedding.

Lehenga

The lehenga is a popular Indian wedding dress worn by brides. It consists of a long skirt, blouse, and a dupatta (a long scarf). The skirt is usually heavily embroidered and can be made of various fabrics such as silk, satin, or chiffon. The blouse can be either short or long-sleeved and can also be heavily embroidered. The dupatta is draped over the head during the wedding ceremony.

My personal experience with the lehenga was when I wore it for my cousin's wedding. I chose a red silk lehenga with gold embroidery, which is a traditional color for Indian brides. The lehenga was heavy and took some getting used to, but I felt like a princess on my cousin's special day.

Sherwani

The sherwani is a traditional Indian wedding dress worn by grooms. It is a long coat-like garment that is worn over a kurta (a long shirt) and churidar (tight-fitting pants). The sherwani is usually made of silk or velvet and can be heavily embroidered.

My brother wore a navy blue silk sherwani for his wedding. The sherwani was embroidered with gold thread and had a matching turban. He looked regal and handsome on his special day.

Saree

The saree is a versatile Indian wedding dress worn by brides. It consists of a long piece of fabric that is draped around the body, with one end draped over the head. The saree can be made of various fabrics such as silk, cotton, or chiffon, and can be heavily embroidered.

The saree is my personal favorite Indian wedding dress. I wore a pink silk saree with gold embroidery for my best friend's wedding. The saree was comfortable to wear and made me feel elegant and graceful.

How to Choose the Right Indian Wedding Dress

Choosing the right Indian wedding dress can be overwhelming, but there are a few things to consider that can make the process easier. Firstly, consider the season and location of the wedding. For example, a heavy silk lehenga may not be suitable for a summer outdoor wedding. Secondly, consider the cultural and religious background of the bride and groom. This can influence the choice of dress and color. Finally, choose a dress that makes you feel confident and comfortable.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the significance of the colors worn in Indian wedding dresses?

A: The colors worn in Indian wedding dresses have different meanings depending on the region and culture. For example, red is a traditional color for Indian brides as it symbolizes love and strength.

Q: Can Indian wedding dresses be rented or borrowed?

A: Yes, Indian wedding dresses can be rented or borrowed. This is a cost-effective option for those who do not want to purchase a dress they may only wear once.

Q: Are Indian wedding dresses only worn during the wedding ceremony?

A: No, Indian wedding dresses can be worn during other pre-wedding and post-wedding ceremonies such as the sangeet (musical evening) and reception.

Q: What is the difference between a lehenga and a ghagra?

A: The lehenga and ghagra are both types of skirts worn by Indian brides. The main difference is that the lehenga is more heavily embroidered and is usually worn with a long-sleeved blouse and a dupatta, while the ghagra is less heavily embroidered and is usually worn with a shorter blouse.

Conclusion of Indian Wedding Dress Name

Indian wedding dress names may seem confusing at first, but with a little research, you can gain a better understanding of their significance. Remember to choose a dress that makes you feel comfortable and confident on your special day. We hope this guide has been helpful in your search for the perfect Indian wedding dress.
