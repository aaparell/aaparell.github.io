If you're someone who loves to shop for clothes online, you must have come across the dilemma of choosing the right size. The struggle is real, and it can be frustrating to receive a package of clothes and find out that the size you ordered doesn't fit you. One of the most common questions that people ask when it comes to shirt sizes is whether size 38 is medium or large.

When it comes to clothes, finding the right size is crucial. Wearing clothes that don't fit properly can make you feel uncomfortable and self-conscious. It can also affect your posture and cause health problems in the long run. Moreover, returning clothes that don't fit can be a hassle, and it can take up a lot of time and effort.

The answer to whether shirt size 38 is medium or large can vary depending on the brand and the country where the shirt was manufactured. However, in general, shirt size 38 is considered a medium size in the US and Europe. In the UK, size 38 is equivalent to a small size. In Asian countries, such as Japan and South Korea, a size 38 is considered a large size.

To sum up, shirt size 38 can be considered medium or large depending on the brand and the country where it was made. However, in general, it is more commonly known as a medium size in the US and Europe.

Understanding Shirt Sizes and Measurements

Shirt sizes can be confusing since they vary between brands and countries. Moreover, different brands have different sizing charts, which can make it challenging to find the right size. To avoid this confusion, it's essential to understand the measurements used to determine shirt sizes.

Shirt sizes are determined by measuring the circumference of the neck and the width of the chest. The neck measurement is taken at the base of the neck, while the chest measurement is taken at the widest part of the chest. Once these measurements are taken, they are compared to a sizing chart to determine the right size.

How to Choose the Right Shirt Size

Choosing the right shirt size can be tricky, especially if you're shopping online. Here are some tips to help you find the right size:

1. Use a Tape Measure

Using a tape measure to measure your neck and chest circumference can give you a better idea of your shirt size. Once you have your measurements, compare them to the sizing chart provided by the brand to determine the right size.

2. Check the Sizing Chart

Always check the sizing chart provided by the brand before buying a shirt. Different brands have different sizing charts, so it's essential to check the chart to make sure you're buying the right size.

3. Read Reviews

Reading reviews from other customers who have bought the same shirt can give you an idea of how the shirt fits. Look for reviews from people who have a similar body type to yours to get a better understanding of whether the shirt will fit you well.
