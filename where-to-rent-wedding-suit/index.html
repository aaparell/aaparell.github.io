Where To Rent Wedding Suit A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Daniel May 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Whether you're the groom, best man, or a guest, finding the perfect suit for a wedding can be a daunting task. It's important to look sharp and put together, but the cost of purchasing a suit can add up quickly. That's where renting a wedding suit comes in, but where do you even start?

If you're wondering where to rent a wedding suit, the good news is that there are plenty of options available. From traditional tuxedo rental shops to online rental services, you can find a suit that fits your style and budget.

The first step in renting a wedding suit is deciding on the style you want. Are you looking for a classic black tuxedo, a trendy navy suit, or something more unique? Once you have a general idea of what you're looking for, it's time to start your search.

To make your search easier, we've compiled a list of the best places to rent a wedding suit. Keep reading to find out more!

1. Men's Wearhouse

Men's Wearhouse is a popular choice for wedding suit rentals. With over 700 locations across the country, you can easily find a store near you. They offer a wide range of styles and sizes, and their expert stylists can help you find the perfect suit for your body type and personal taste.

Personally, I used Men's Wearhouse for my brother's wedding and had a great experience. The staff was friendly and knowledgeable, and the suit fit perfectly.

2. The Black Tux

If you're looking for a more modern and trendy suit, The Black Tux is the perfect option. They offer a variety of stylish options, from classic tuxedos to slim-fit suits in unique colors and patterns. The best part? You can rent everything online, making the process super convenient.

A friend of mine used The Black Tux for his wedding and was extremely happy with the quality and fit of the suit. Plus, the online rental process was quick and easy.

3. Generation Tux

Generation Tux is another great option for renting a wedding suit online. They offer a wide variety of styles and colors, and their prices are competitive. Plus, they offer free home try-ons, so you can make sure the suit fits properly before the wedding day.

I haven't personally used Generation Tux, but I've heard great things from friends who have. They offer a wide range of sizes, which is especially helpful for those who have trouble finding suits that fit properly.

4. Jos. A. Bank

Jos. A. Bank is a well-known menswear store that also offers wedding suit rentals. They have a variety of styles and sizes available, and their prices are reasonable. Plus, their expert stylists can help you find the perfect suit for your body type and personal taste.

I used Jos. A. Bank for my own wedding and was happy with the quality and fit of the suit. The staff was friendly and helpful, and the rental process was quick and easy.

Question and Answer:

Q: How much does it cost to rent a wedding suit?

A: The cost of renting a wedding suit varies depending on the style, brand, and location. On average, you can expect to spend anywhere from $100 to $300 on a rental.

Q: How far in advance should I rent a wedding suit?

A: It's recommended that you rent your wedding suit at least 4-6 weeks before the wedding day to ensure that everything fits properly and you have time for any necessary alterations.

Q: What should I look for when renting a wedding suit?

A: When renting a wedding suit, you should look for a style that fits your body type and personal taste, as well as a rental company with good customer reviews and a wide range of sizes and styles available.

Q: Can I rent a wedding suit online?

A: Yes, there are several online rental companies that offer wedding suit rentals, such as The Black Tux and Generation Tux.

Conclusion of Where to Rent Wedding Suit:

When it comes to finding the perfect wedding suit, renting is a great option that can save you time and money. By using the tips and recommendations in this guide, you can find a suit that fits your style and budget, and feel confident and put together on the big day.
