Mens Fashion In The 1920S Canada A Guide To The Roaring Twenties Style

Written by Christine Oct 26, 2023 · 5 min read

The 1920s was a time of change, liberation, and celebration of life. The Canadian society was no exception, and mens fashion in the 1920s Canada reflected this shift towards a more liberal and modern way of life. However, finding the right style and fit could be challenging, and many men struggled to keep up with the latest trends. In this post, we will guide you through the mens fashion in the 1920s Canada, and help you achieve the perfect Roaring Twenties style!

The mens fashion in the 1920s Canada was all about breaking free from the strict and conservative Victorian era. Men wanted to express their individuality and personality through their clothing, and the Roaring Twenties style allowed them to do just that. However, not everyone had the means to afford the latest fashion, and some men had to make do with what they had. This led to a blend of old and new styles, creating a unique and distinct look.

The target of mens fashion in the 1920s Canada was to create a relaxed and comfortable style that was also fashionable and modern. Men wanted to look good and feel good, and the Roaring Twenties style allowed them to do just that. The popular clothing items included suits, trousers, shirts, ties, hats, and shoes. The fabrics were lightweight and breathable, making them suitable for the summer heat.

In summary, mens fashion in the 1920s Canada was all about breaking free from the strict and conservative Victorian era, and creating a relaxed and comfortable style that was also fashionable and modern. The target was to look good and feel good, and the popular clothing items included suits, trousers, shirts, ties, hats, and shoes. The fabrics were lightweight and breathable, making them suitable for the summer heat.

The Suits:

One of the defining features of mens fashion in the 1920s Canada was the suit. The suits were single-breasted, with narrow lapels, and straight-cut trousers. The jackets were short and snug-fitting, with padded shoulders and high armholes. The fabrics were lightweight and breathable, with pinstripes, herringbone, and checks being popular choices. The suits were often paired with contrasting waistcoats and colorful ties, creating a bold and daring look.

My grandfather used to tell me stories about how he used to wear his pinstripe suit to the local dance hall. The suit made him feel confident and stylish, and he always got compliments from the ladies. He would pair his suit with a colorful tie and his favorite hat, and dance the night away.

The Trousers:

The trousers in mens fashion in the 1920s Canada were high-waisted, with narrow legs, and cuffs at the bottom. They were often made from wool or tweed, and came in various colors such as grey, brown, and blue. The trousers were paired with a shirt and tie, and sometimes a waistcoat or a sweater. The trousers were also worn with suspenders, which were both functional and fashionable.

I remember my great-grandfather telling me about how he used to wear his high-waisted trousers to work in the fields. The trousers were durable and comfortable, and allowed him to move around freely. He would pair them with a simple shirt and suspenders, and spend his day working on the farm.

The Shirts:

The shirts in mens fashion in the 1920s Canada were often made from lightweight and breathable fabrics, such as cotton or linen. They came in various colors and patterns, such as stripes, checks, and solids. The collars were often detachable, allowing men to change them depending on the occasion. The shirts were tucked into the trousers, and sometimes worn with a sweater or a waistcoat.

The Hats:

The hats in mens fashion in the 1920s Canada were an essential accessory. They came in various styles, such as fedoras, trilbies, and Homburgs. The hats were made from felt or straw, and often had a ribbon or a band around them. The hats were worn tilted to one side, creating a relaxed and casual look.

I remember my uncle telling me about how he used to wear his fedora to the local jazz club. The hat made him feel like a true gentleman, and he would often pair it with a colorful tie and his favorite suit. He would dance to the music, and the hat would tilt to one side, making him look even more stylish.

Q&A:

Q: What were the popular colors in mens fashion in the 1920s Canada?
A: The popular colors were grey, brown, blue, and various shades of green and purple.

Q: What kind of shoes were worn in mens fashion in the 1920s Canada?
A: The shoes were often two-toned, with a leather or suede upper and a rubber sole. The popular styles were oxfords, brogues, and loafers.

Q: What kind of accessories were worn in mens fashion in the 1920s Canada?
A: The popular accessories were ties, pocket squares, cufflinks, and watches. The accessories were often colorful and bold, creating a contrast with the neutral colors of the clothing.

Q: What kind of hairstyles were popular in mens fashion in the 1920s Canada?
A: The popular hairstyles were short and slicked back, with a side parting. The hair was often styled with pomade or oil, creating a shiny and smooth look.

Conclusion of Mens Fashion in the 1920s Canada:

Mens fashion in the 1920s Canada was all about breaking free from the strict and conservative Victorian era, and creating a relaxed and comfortable style that was also fashionable and modern. The popular clothing items included suits, trousers, shirts, ties, hats, and shoes. The fabrics were lightweight and breathable, making them suitable for the summer heat. The Roaring Twenties style allowed men to express their individuality and personality through their clothing, and created a unique and distinct look. We hope this guide has helped you achieve the perfect Roaring Twenties style!
