Best Up And Coming Fashion Brands You Need To Know About

Written by Christine Jul 03, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of the same old fashion brands? Do you want to discover new and exciting labels that are making waves in the industry? Look no further, as we've compiled a list of the best up and coming fashion brands that you need to know about.

It can be overwhelming to keep up with the ever-changing world of fashion. With so many established brands dominating the market, it can be difficult for emerging designers to get noticed. However, there are a number of up and coming fashion brands that are challenging the status quo and bringing fresh ideas to the table.

If you're looking for new and exciting fashion labels to add to your wardrobe, we've got you covered. Below, we'll highlight the best up and coming fashion brands that are making waves in the industry.

From sustainable streetwear to high-end couture, these brands are redefining what it means to be fashionable. Whether you're looking for timeless classics or bold statement pieces, these labels offer something for everyone.

1. Nanushka

Nanushka is a Hungarian fashion label that has been making waves in the industry in recent years. Founded by designer Sandra Sandor in 2005, the brand is known for its luxurious yet affordable designs that blend Eastern and Western influences.

One of the brand's signature pieces is the "vegan leather" puffer jacket, which has become a cult favorite among fashion insiders. Nanushka's designs are perfect for those who want to make a statement without sacrificing comfort or affordability.

2. A-Cold-Wall

A-Cold-Wall is a British streetwear label that was founded by designer Samuel Ross in 2015. The brand is known for its minimalist designs that blend high-end fashion with streetwear aesthetics.

A-Cold-Wall has quickly gained a cult following thanks to its unique designs and collaborations with brands such as Nike and Converse. If you're looking for edgy and experimental streetwear, A-Cold-Wall is the brand for you.

3. Marine Serre

Marine Serre is a French fashion designer who launched her eponymous label in 2017. The brand is known for its futuristic designs that incorporate sustainable materials and upcycling techniques.

Serre's designs have been worn by the likes of Beyoncé and Kendall Jenner, and the brand has received critical acclaim for its innovative approach to fashion. If you're looking for sustainable fashion that doesn't sacrifice style, Marine Serre is the brand for you.

4. Thebe Magugu

Thebe Magugu is a South African fashion designer who launched his eponymous label in 2016. The brand is known for its bold and colorful designs that draw inspiration from African culture and history.

Thebe Magugu has quickly gained a following in the fashion world thanks to its unique designs and commitment to sustainability. The brand's collections are all made in South Africa and incorporate locally sourced materials.

Question and Answer:

Q: Where can I buy these up and coming fashion brands?

A: You can find these brands on their respective websites or through select retailers that carry their collections.

Q: Are these brands affordable?

A: While some of these brands may be more expensive than fast fashion labels, they are still affordable compared to high-end luxury brands.

Q: Do these brands offer sustainable options?

A: Yes, many of these up and coming fashion brands are committed to sustainability and offer eco-friendly options.

Q: Are these brands available internationally?

A: Yes, many of these brands have a global presence and offer international shipping.

Conclusion of Best Up and Coming Fashion Brands

If you're looking to shake up your wardrobe and discover new and exciting fashion labels, these up and coming brands are a great place to start. From sustainable streetwear to high-end couture, these brands offer something for everyone.

By supporting up and coming fashion brands, you're not only discovering unique and innovative designs, but you're also supporting independent designers who are challenging the status quo and bringing fresh ideas to the industry.
