Types of Indian Groom Wear: A Guide for the Modern Groom

Getting married is one of the biggest milestones in one's life. As a groom, you want to look your best on your big day. However, choosing the right outfit can be overwhelming, especially if you are not familiar with the different types of Indian groom wear. With so many options available, it can be challenging to know where to start. In this post, we'll explore the different types of Indian groom wear, their target audience, and what makes them unique.

If you're a groom-to-be, you want to feel comfortable and confident on your wedding day. Your wedding outfit should reflect your personal style while still adhering to the traditions of your culture. One of the biggest challenges that grooms face when choosing their wedding outfit is striking a balance between fashion and tradition. You want to wear something that looks stylish while still being respectful of your culture and heritage.

So, what are the different types of Indian groom wear? Here are some of the most popular options:

Sherwani

Sherwani is a popular choice for grooms who want to look regal and elegant on their wedding day. It is a long coat-like garment that is buttoned up to the collar and worn with a churidar or dhoti. Sherwani is typically made of silk, satin, or brocade and features intricate embroidery or embellishments.

When I got married, I chose to wear a sherwani because it was the perfect combination of traditional and modern. The intricate embroidery and embellishments added a touch of elegance to my outfit, while the modern cut made it look stylish and trendy.

Kurta Pyjama

Kurta pyjama is a more casual option for grooms who want to keep things simple and comfortable. It is a loose-fitting, long tunic worn over pajama-style pants. Kurta pyjama is typically made of cotton or silk and features minimal embroidery or embellishments.

My brother opted for a kurta pyjama on his wedding day because he wanted to feel comfortable and relaxed. He looked effortlessly stylish in his simple yet elegant outfit.

Sherwani Suit

A sherwani suit is a fusion of the traditional sherwani and a western suit. It is a modern take on the classic sherwani and is perfect for grooms who want to look stylish and modern. A sherwani suit typically features a jacket-style top with a western-style collar and lapels, worn over a pair of trousers.

My friend chose to wear a sherwani suit on his wedding day because he wanted to look stylish and modern while still honoring his culture and traditions. The fusion of the western suit and traditional sherwani made for a unique and trendy outfit.

Bandhgala

Bandhgala, also known as a Jodhpuri suit, is a modern take on the classic Indian suit. It is a tailored suit with a high-collared jacket and slim-fitting trousers. Bandhgala is typically made of silk or cotton and features minimal embroidery or embellishments.

My cousin wore a bandhgala on his wedding day because he wanted to look sharp and sophisticated. The slim-fitting trousers and tailored jacket gave him a modern and trendy look, while the high collar added a touch of elegance and sophistication.

Question and Answer

Q: What type of Indian groom wear is best for a summer wedding?

A: For a summer wedding, it's best to opt for lighter fabrics like cotton or linen. A kurta pyjama or a sherwani in a light fabric will keep you cool and comfortable during the hot weather.

Q: What type of Indian groom wear is best for a winter wedding?

A: For a winter wedding, it's best to opt for heavier fabrics like silk or velvet. A sherwani or a bandhgala in a rich fabric will keep you warm and stylish during the cold weather.

Q: Can I wear a western-style suit on my wedding day?

A: While it's not traditional, it's not uncommon for grooms to wear a western-style suit on their wedding day. However, if you want to honor your culture and traditions, it's best to opt for one of the traditional Indian groom wear options.

Q: Should I wear a turban with my Indian groom wear?

A: It's not mandatory to wear a turban with your Indian groom wear, but it's a traditional accessory that can add a touch of elegance to your outfit. If you're not comfortable wearing a turban, you can opt for a stylish hat or headpiece instead.

Conclusion of Types of Indian Groom Wear

Choosing the right outfit for your wedding day can be overwhelming, but with this guide, you should have a better understanding of the different types of Indian groom wear available. Whether you opt for a traditional sherwani or a modern sherwani suit, make sure your outfit reflects your personal style while still honoring your culture and traditions.
