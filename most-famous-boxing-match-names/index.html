The Rumble in the Jungle

The Rumble in the Jungle is one of the most famous boxing match names that took place on October 30, 1974, in Kinshasa, Zaire. The fight was between George Foreman and Muhammad Ali and was the heavyweight championship match of the world.

As for the target, the match was Ali's attempt to regain the world heavyweight championship he had lost to Joe Frazier three years earlier. Foreman, on the other hand, was the reigning champion and was considered unbeatable.

The match itself was a spectacle, with Ali using his famous "rope-a-dope" technique to wear out Foreman, who was known for his powerful punches. In the end, Ali won the match, becoming the second man in history to win the world heavyweight championship three times.

The Thrilla in Manila

The Thrilla in Manila is another famous boxing match name that took place on October 1, 1975, in Manila, the Philippines. The match was between Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier and was the third and final match between the two heavyweight champions.

The target of the match was to settle the score between Ali and Frazier, who had split their first two matches. The fight was brutal, with both boxers giving it their all. In the end, Ali emerged victorious, but not before Frazier's trainer threw in the towel, unable to watch his fighter take any more punishment.

The Fight of the Century

The Fight of the Century is a famous boxing match name that took place on March 8, 1971, at Madison Square Garden in New York City. The match was between Muhammad Ali and Joe Frazier and was the first time two undefeated heavyweight champions had fought each other.

The target of the match was to determine who was the best heavyweight boxer in the world. The fight was close, with both boxers giving their all. However, in the end, Frazier emerged victorious, handing Ali his first professional loss.

The Battle of the Sexes

The Battle of the Sexes is a famous boxing match name that took place on September 20, 1973, in Houston, Texas. The match was between Billie Jean King, a female tennis player, and Bobby Riggs, a former men's tennis champion.

The target of the match was to prove that women could compete with men. King won the match in straight sets, proving that women were just as capable as men in sports.
