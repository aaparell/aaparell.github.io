Waltzing Matilda

One of the most iconic classic Australian songs about Australia is "Waltzing Matilda". This folk song tells the story of a swagman who steals a sheep and is pursued by the police. The swagman eventually chooses to drown himself in a nearby billabong rather than be captured. The song has become so popular that it is often considered the unofficial national anthem of Australia.

My personal experience with "Waltzing Matilda" comes from my childhood. I remember singing this song in school and feeling a sense of pride in my country's history and culture. The lyrics are simple yet powerful, and they have a way of connecting people to the land and to each other.

I Am Australian

"I Am Australian" is another classic Australian song about Australia that celebrates the country's diversity and multiculturalism. The song was written in 1987 and has become a beloved anthem for many Australians. The lyrics speak to the idea that no matter where someone comes from, they can still feel a sense of belonging in Australia.

As someone who has lived in Australia for many years but was not born here, "I Am Australian" holds a special place in my heart. The song reminds me that even though I may not have been born in Australia, I can still feel a sense of pride and belonging in this beautiful country.
